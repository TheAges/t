Brief
In collaboration with Frankfurt Messe, our team at Runelab developed an access control system for event staff and a guest-exhibitor application for networking and event management. With just three months before the SPS 2022 exhibition, I led the design process under tight deadlines. I focused on creating efficient check-ins, real-time event tracking, smooth exhibitor-guest interactions, and intuitive event navigation. The result was a set of user-friendly solutions that met the client's needs and delivered seamless experiences for both staff and attendees.

The Challenge & Design Process

For the SPS Italia Smart Production Solutions exhibition of 2022, Frankfurt Messe partnered with Runelab to develop a digital solution for event access control and guest-exhibitor interactions. However, with only three months before the event, there was an urgent need to implement a functional system.

The key challenges were:

  • Lack of a Ticketing & Access Control System
    Event staff needed a fast and reliable tool to verify tickets and manage guest flow at entry points, connected to a backend for real-time tracking.
  • Guest-Exhibitor Interaction Under COVID Restrictions
    Traditional networking methods were limited, requiring a digital platform to facilitate exhibitor-guest connections, event bookings, and information sharing while maintaining safety protocols.

With the short timeline, the goal was to design and deploy a seamless, intuitive experience that would integrate with Frankfurt Messe's existing infrastructure.

Given the extremely limited timeframe, a full user-centered design approach was not feasible. Instead, we adopted an efficient and pragmatic methodology that prioritized:

  • Stakeholder-Driven Design
    Close collaboration with Frankfurt Messe to gather essential requirements and align the solutions with their operational needs.
  • Heuristic & Best Practices Approach
    Relying on established UX/UI patterns from event management, ticketing, and networking applications to accelerate design decisions.
  • Rapid Prototyping & Iteration
    Quickly developing functional wireframes and conducting short design reviews with the client to validate concepts before finalizing designs.
  • Technical Feasibility-First Approach
    Designing solutions that could be developed and deployed quickly within the constraints of Frankfurt Messe's infrastructure.

This agile approach allowed us to align the technical constraints, event logistics, and user experience goals within the short timeline.

Access Control App & Backend Control Platform

The staff-facing application was designed with a focus on speed and simplicity, ensuring quick and seamless ticket validation at event entry points. The app enabled staff to quickly scan QR codes, streamlining the process and significantly reducing wait times for attendees. The user interface of the app was kept minimal and intuitive, ensuring that even with little to no training, event staff could use the system effectively. The goal was to provide a reliable and user-friendly tool that staff could depend on, even under high-pressure conditions.

Staff-facing access control app: fast QR ticket validation at the gates.

To ensure smooth operations, we worked closely with the client to design the error flow and manage potential issues at the gates. Different scenarios, such as invalid tickets or system errors, were carefully mapped out to ensure staff could address problems quickly and efficiently. This process minimized disruptions and maintained a seamless experience for attendees.

Staff-facing access control app: the validation flow, with the individual cases the client asked us to handle.

The app was integrated with a real-time backend platform, allowing event organizers to track attendance and manage guest entries dynamically. This integration ensured that the system could handle a large volume of entries while maintaining accurate and up-to-date data. Additionally, the backend provided valuable statistical insights, including visitor data and entry trends, which helped organizers optimize event management and track performance in real-time.

Backend platform: access control, event data, and visitor management.

The backend platform was also developed with future events in mind. It was designed to be scalable and customizable, allowing the system to be adapted for different events and evolving client needs. This ensured that the solution could grow with the client and remain relevant for future use cases.

Guest & Exhibitor Application

The guest-exhibitor app served as a centralized platform for event attendees, designed to improve engagement and facilitate networking while ensuring compliance with COVID-19 restrictions. The app allowed both guests and exhibitors to interact seamlessly, streamlining event operations and enhancing the attendee experience.

One of the core functionalities of the app was digital ticket management, allowing guests to access, validate, and check their event status directly on their mobile devices. This feature eliminated the need for physical ticket exchanges, reducing contact points and improving the check-in process. Additionally, the app enabled exhibitor-guest interactions, allowing guests to easily exchange contact details and company information. This made networking easier while keeping interactions touchless, which was critical due to the ongoing pandemic.

Guest and exhibitor app: extract from the login flow.

The app also included features for event booking and scheduling, where guests could pre-book meetings with exhibitors and manage their personal event itinerary. This allowed for a smoother flow of interactions, ensuring that guests could plan their time efficiently and take full advantage of the event. On-site navigation was also integrated into the app, with an interactive map that helped attendees locate booths, event spaces, and conference sessions, ensuring that they could navigate the venue without difficulty.

Guest and exhibitor app: networking, digital tickets, and on-site navigation.

To keep attendees informed, the app included a feature for event promotion and live updates. Organizers were able to send real-time notifications about workshops, talks, and schedule changes, ensuring that guests were always up to date on important event information.

The visual design of the app was carefully aligned with Frankfurt Messe's brand identity and communication guidelines, maintaining consistency across all digital touchpoints.

Outcome & Impact

Despite the tight three-month deadline, the project was successfully delivered and deployed before the event, ensuring smooth operations and enhanced attendee engagement. As the lead designer, I was responsible for defining the UX/UI strategy, user flows, wireframes, and high-fidelity prototypes within an extremely short timeframe. By prioritizing rapid prototyping, industry benchmarking, and close collaboration with stakeholders, I ensured that both solutions were delivered on time, met the client's expectations, and provided a functional, scalable digital experience for the event.
